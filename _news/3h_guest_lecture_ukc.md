On **3 June 2026**, I delivered an in-person guest lecture at the [University of Kent](https://www.kent.ac.uk/) for the master's module [COMP7029: Artificial Intelligence and Cyber Security](https://www.kent.ac.uk/courses/modules/module/COMP7029). The lecture took place at **9:00 AM** in [Sibson](https://www.kent.ac.uk/conferences-and-events/venues/sibson) Seminar Room 7 and was attended by around **20 engaged master's students**.

I was invited by [Dr Rogério de Lemos](https://www.kent.ac.uk/school-of-computing/people/3151/de-lemos-rogerio), Senior Lecturer and Director of Postgraduate Research (PGR) in the [School of Computing](https://www.kent.ac.uk/school-of-computing).

The lecture, **"From Chatbot to Cyber Agent: Governing AI that acts on cyber infrastructure,"** explored how the security problem changes when AI systems move from simply answering questions to taking actions through tools. I introduced the idea of cyber agents, their opportunities and risks, and why governance is essential when AI systems interact with cyber infrastructure.

The lecture was based on my recent work, [APIOT: Autonomous Vulnerability Management Across Bare-Metal Industrial OT Networks](/assets/pdf/apiot.pdf), which I used as the main case study to discuss autonomous vulnerability management in industrial OT networks. I also shared examples from the slides on how such systems can be evaluated across discovery, exploitation, patching, and verification tasks involving protocols such as CoAP, Modbus/TCP, and MQTT.

The session also included a short in-class exercise where students reflected on how to set boundaries for an AI agent scanning a small network of vulnerable IoT devices and recommending fixes. It was a pleasure to discuss this work with the COMP7029 students and see their engagement with questions around AI, cyber security, and responsible autonomy.
